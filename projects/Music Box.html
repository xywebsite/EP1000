Music Box

I learnt about materials that I can use to cut with the laser, for example, wood and acrylic.

I also learnt about materials that i should not cut with the laser, such as epoxy, PVC and more, as it would release dangerous fumes and would damage the laser.

We were then tasked to laser cut a music box that required

- a moveable lid

- an area large enough to hold the music box mechanism.

- must be drawn and modelled in Fusion 360

- must have decoration on the box

- must be able to accommodate the hand crank at the side of the box

- must be conneccted without nails

My sketch

I decided to make a sketch of the music box so the process of making the box in Fusion 360 would be easier.

Music Box in Fusion 360

Preparing the Fusion 360 model for laser cutting.

I exported the file as an AutoCAD DXF, Data exchange format.

The software i used to check and finalize my cuts was Adobe Illustrator for the final cutting and engravings.

I isolated the 2D profile of the object face.

Then, I exported the profile as a DXF file.

Then, I imported the DXF file into Adobe Illustrator.

I then made the necessary changes and adjustments in Adobe Illustrator, such as changing the thickness by adjusting the outlines.

I also added the decorations to the box faces in Adobe Illustrator.

Laser cutting : line stroke < or = to 0.025 line colour RED

Laser scoring : line stroke < or = to 0.025 line colour GREEN

Laser engraving : fill colour or line stroke >0.025 line colour GREEN

Send the profile to the lasercutter to cut out.
